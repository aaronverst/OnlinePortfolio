Reflection

My lifelong passion for technology and computers led me to pursuing a computer science degree at the University of Cincinnati. Since my enrollment, I have completed various co-op experiences and projects that have enhanced my skills as a coder and problem solver. My co-op experiences have been influencial in sharpening my collaboration skills and my professional development has been accelerated. However, that's not to say that my co-op experiences weren't tough at times. By deploying my skills in a professional environement, I was subject to critique and criticism by team members regarding projects and tasks that I was assigned. This ultimately lead me towards becoming a better collaborator and overall developer. I was surprised at how much I was able to learn while on co-op. Co-op showed me that I enjoy learning through experience and trial rather than through books and theory. If I had to do my co-op journey over again, I would have done more research on the career paths available in computer science. Prior to my first co-op experience I was unaware of how broad the field was and how many oppurtunities exist within programming and development. In my remaining co-op terms I intend to explore this realization and expose myself to new areas of computer science which I am unfamiliar with. I hope that this will lead me in to discovering new hobbies and possibly even a career path.
